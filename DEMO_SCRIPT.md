# DEMO SCRIPT: PW Plant Management System
## Live Walkthrough Guide

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes
**Audience:** [Manager's Name], Plant Officer, Management
**Goal:** Show the system solves their exact problems with their actual data

---

## PRE-DEMO CHECKLIST

Before the meeting:

- [ ] System is running and accessible (test login 30 min before)
- [ ] Load real P.W. data — at least 2-3 weeks of weekly reports processed
- [ ] Have 10+ purchase orders entered with real supplier names
- [ ] Have at least 3-4 plant transfers recorded
- [ ] Know 5-6 specific fleet numbers by heart (to search live)
- [ ] Have the proposal document printed (2-3 copies)
- [ ] Charge your laptop fully, have backup mobile hotspot for internet
- [ ] Clear browser history/tabs — only the app should be visible

---

## THE DEMO (Step by Step)

### OPENING (2 minutes)

**Do NOT start with the computer. Start with conversation.**

> "Sir, thank you for your time. Before I show you anything, let me ask a quick question.
>
> If I called you right now and said — 'How many excavators does P.W. have across all sites, and which ones are currently on breakdown?' — how long would it take to get me that answer?"

*Wait for their response. They'll likely say hours/days or admit they can't easily.*

> "What I've built gives you that answer in 3 seconds. Let me show you."

**Now open the laptop.**

---

### SCENE 1: THE DASHBOARD (3 minutes)

**What to show:** The main dashboard

**Talk track:**
> "This is your command center. At a glance, you can see:"

Point to each element:
- **Total plants** — "You have [X] plants registered in the system"
- **Condition breakdown** — "Right now, [X] are working, [X] on standby, [X] on breakdown"
- **Plants by location** — "Here's how they're distributed across your sites"
- **Recent activity** — "These are the latest movements and updates"

**Key moment:**
> "Everything you see here updates in real-time. When a weekly report comes in from any site, this dashboard reflects it immediately."

---

### SCENE 2: "WHERE IS MY PLANT?" (3 minutes)

**This is the most important demo. The manager specifically asked for this.**

**Talk track:**
> "Sir, give me any fleet number — any plant you want to know about."

*Let them pick a real fleet number. If they can't think of one, have 2-3 ready.*

**Actions:**
1. Type the fleet number in the search bar
2. Click on the plant
3. Show the plant detail page:
   - Current location
   - Current condition (working/standby/breakdown)
   - Fleet type and description
   - **Movement history** — "Here's every location this plant has been at, with dates"
   - **Maintenance costs** — "This is how much you've spent maintaining this specific plant"
   - **Weekly usage** — hours worked, standby hours, breakdown hours

**Key moment:**
> "This is what 'hands-on report at any given time' looks like. Any plant, any time, full history."

---

### SCENE 3: SITE/LOCATION VIEW (2 minutes)

**Talk track:**
> "Now let's look at it from a site perspective. Let's say you want to know what's at [Site Name]."

**Actions:**
1. Click on Sites in the sidebar
2. Click on a specific location
3. Show:
   - Total plants at that site
   - Breakdown by condition (working/standby/breakdown)
   - List of every plant currently there
   - Fleet type distribution

**Key moment:**
> "Before this, getting a complete picture of one site meant calling the site officer and waiting. Now it's one click."

---

### SCENE 4: WEEKLY REPORT PROCESSING (3 minutes)

**Talk track:**
> "You know how every site sends a weekly Excel report? Currently someone has to open each file, manually check the data, compile it. With 30+ sites, that's 30+ files every week."

**Actions:**
1. Go to Upload page
2. Show how a file is uploaded
3. Show the preview — "The system reads the Excel file and shows you what it found before saving"
4. Show a processed submission — "Here's a report that was already processed. [X] plants extracted, [X] created, [X] updated"

**Key moment:**
> "What used to take someone an entire day — processing 30 weekly reports — the system does in minutes. And it catches errors automatically."

---

### SCENE 5: SPARE PARTS & COST TRACKING (4 minutes)

**This is where the money conversation happens.**

**Talk track:**
> "Now let me show you where the real money is — spare parts and maintenance costs."

**Actions:**
1. Go to **Spare Parts** page — show the list of parts with costs
2. Go to **Purchase Orders** — show PO list with amounts
3. Click on a specific PO — show itemized breakdown
4. Go to **Parts Analytics** page:
   - Point to **Total Cost card** — "This is your total spare parts spend for [year]"
   - Point to **Monthly Trend chart** — "You can see which months had the highest spend"
   - Point to **Year-over-Year chart** — "Compare this year vs last year"
   - Point to **Top Suppliers** — "These are your top 10 suppliers by spend"
   - Point to **Highest Cost Plants** — "These 10 plants are costing you the most to maintain"

**Key moment:**
> "Right now, can anyone in the company tell you which 10 plants cost the most to maintain? Or which supplier you're spending the most with? This system gives you that instantly. This is how you negotiate better rates, decide what to repair vs replace, and stop overspending."

---

### SCENE 6: PLANT TRANSFERS (2 minutes)

**Talk track:**
> "When a plant moves from one site to another, it needs to be tracked. Otherwise equipment gets 'lost' between sites."

**Actions:**
1. Go to Transfers page
2. Show a transfer record — from location, to location, date, status
3. Show how a transfer updates the plant's location

**Key moment:**
> "Every movement is recorded. If a plant leaves Site A, it shows up at Site B. Nothing falls through the cracks."

---

### SCENE 7: REPORTS (3 minutes)

**Talk track:**
> "Management needs reports. Let me show you what's available at one click."

**Actions (move quickly through each):**
1. **Fleet Summary** — Bar chart showing fleet breakdown by type across all locations
2. **Maintenance Costs** — Cost analysis with flexible grouping (by month, by location, by fleet type)
3. **Verification Status** — Which locations are verifying their plants, which aren't
4. **Weekly Trends** — Plant count trends and verification rates over time

**Key moment:**
> "Any of these reports can be pulled up in seconds during a management meeting. No preparation needed."

---

### SCENE 8: SUPPLIERS (1 minute)

**Actions:**
1. Go to Suppliers page
2. Show supplier list with PO count and total spend
3. Click on one supplier — show their full purchase history

**Key moment:**
> "Complete supplier relationship history. Useful for audits, negotiations, and decision-making."

---

### SCENE 9: SECURITY & ACCESS CONTROL (1 minute)

**Talk track:**
> "The system has two access levels: Admin and Management. Management users can view everything and pull reports. Admin users can also upload data, manage users, and make changes. Everything is password-protected, and every action is logged."

**Actions:**
1. Briefly show the Users page (admin section)
2. Briefly show the Audit Log — "Every action is recorded — who did what, when"

---

### CLOSING (5 minutes)

**Do NOT rush this part. This is where you sell.**

> "Sir, let me summarize what you've just seen:
>
> 1. **Instant fleet visibility** — any plant, any time, full history
> 2. **Automated report processing** — weekly reports from 30+ sites processed in minutes
> 3. **Cost intelligence** — know exactly where your money goes on spare parts
> 4. **Movement tracking** — no more lost or unaccounted equipment
> 5. **Management reports** — one-click access during any meeting
>
> I built this from scratch over 5 months, specifically for P.W. Nigeria. I used your actual weekly reports, your actual purchase orders, your actual fleet data.
>
> An off-the-shelf system like SAP or IBM Maximo would cost N30-100 million per year and take 6-12 months just to set up. And it still wouldn't be customized to how P.W. works.
>
> What I'm proposing is simple: Bring me on formally as your **Digital Solutions & Automation Engineer**. I'll run this system day-to-day, process all the weekly data, generate reports for management, and continuously improve it.
>
> And once this is running smoothly, we move to Phase 2 — **project management automation**. Same approach: understand your workflow, build a system around it, give management instant visibility into project timelines, costs, and progress.
>
> I have a detailed proposal here with the full breakdown."

**Hand over the printed proposal.**

> "I'm happy to answer any questions, or we can schedule a follow-up after you've reviewed it."

## HANDLING OBJECTIONS

### "Can we just have the system and hire someone else to run it?"

> "The system needs someone who understands both the code and the business. I designed every screen around how P.W. operates. A new hire would take 3-6 months to understand the system, and they wouldn't be able to modify or improve it. I built it, I maintain it, and I improve it."

### "This seems expensive."

> "The total annual cost — my salary plus infrastructure — is about N6 million per year. Your company spends more than that on fuel for a single site in one month. The system manages assets worth billions of naira. Even a 1% improvement in equipment utilization pays for this many times over."

### "We need to think about it."

> "Of course. While you review the proposal, I'd suggest I continue processing this week's data so you can see the system with even more live information by our next meeting. The more real data in the system, the more useful it becomes."

### "Can you reduce the setup fee?"

> "I've already discounted it significantly. A software agency would charge N15-40 million for this. I'm asking N3 million because I want a long-term relationship with P.W., not a one-time payment. But I did invest 5 months and my own money building this, so the setup fee is important to me."

### "We already use Excel, it works fine."

> "Excel works for small operations. But with 2,000+ plants across 30+ sites, you're spending hours every week just compiling and reconciling spreadsheets. And you still can't answer basic questions quickly — like where a specific plant is, or which one costs the most. This system takes all those Excel files and turns them into actionable intelligence."

### "What if you leave?"

> "The system is built on standard, well-documented technologies. If I were to leave, any qualified developer could take it over. But my goal is to grow with P.W. — plant management first, then project management, then whatever else the company needs digitized."

## DEMO TIPS

1. **Go slow.** Executives don't want to see you click fast. Pause on each screen. Let them absorb it.

2. **Use THEIR language.** Say "fleet number" not "plant ID." Say "site" not "location." Say "spare parts cost" not "maintenance analytics."

3. **Let them interact.** Ask them to give you a fleet number to search. Ask which site they want to see. The more they participate, the more they trust it's real.

4. **Have real data.** Nothing kills a demo faster than "sample data." Use actual P.W. fleet numbers, actual site names, actual supplier names.

5. **Dress professionally.** You're proposing a N3M+ engagement. Look the part.

6. **Print the proposal.** Don't just email it. Physical documents feel more serious and are harder to ignore.

7. **Bring a notebook.** Write down any feedback or requirements they mention. Shows you're listening and planning ahead.

8. **Don't oversell the tech.** They don't care about React or PostgreSQL. They care about: "Can I see where my plants are? Can I see what I'm spending? Can I get a report right now?"

9. **If something fails during demo:** Stay calm. Say "The system runs on cloud infrastructure, and occasionally there are connectivity delays. Let me show you this other section while it loads." Always have a backup section ready.

10. **End with next steps.** Never end with "so... what do you think?" End with: "I'd like to schedule the formal deployment by [date]. Does that work for you?"
