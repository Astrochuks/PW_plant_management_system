Three things that need your decision

The fuel record is a photocopy

₦14.1m

For seven straight weeks — W3 to W10 — the Akwa per-machine fuel sheet is identical: same 22 machines, same 6,056 litres, week after week. The Cost Report meanwhile shows 61,027 litres bought this year against 49,123 logged. 11,904 litres — ₦14.1m at the price we paid — is diesel no machine is recorded as burning.

DecisionFuel logged per machine, every week, entered at site so it cannot be carried forward — or we keep buying fuel we cannot trace.

We paid a third more per litre in one week

₦1,200 → ₦1,600

Between W9 and W10 the diesel spend rose 44.7% while the litres delivered rose only 8.5%. The rest is price: ₦1,200 a litre became ₦1,600 — a 33% jump in a single week, on the largest single line in our cost report (₦72.2m of AGO in 2026).

DecisionWho approved the new price, and is it one supplier? A ₦400/litre swing on this volume is ₦24m a year.

We are steering on old numbers

140 & 510 days

Akwa's last workbook covers the week ending 6 March 2026. Kaduna's covers 1 March 2025. Everything on this page is true as at those dates — the system is exactly as current as the last report a site sends in.

DecisionInstruct both sites to resume the weekly return, and approve site data entry so a report cannot simply be re-sent unchanged.

One caveat, stated plainly. Both projects carry the same contractual completion date, 18 December 2021 — a workbook template default, not a real date. Overdue months are therefore not quoted on this page; the award letters are needed to correct them. Kaduna has sent no certificate or payment ledger at all, so its certified and retention positions are unknown rather than nil.
